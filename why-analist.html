why analist
@FIN?
“Now I think it’s one of the most useless questions an adult can ask a child—What do you want to be when you grow up? As if growing up is finite. As if at some point you become something and that’s the end.”
― Michelle Obama, Becoming ―
Until a year and a half ago, I never considered working in IT. My only experience with programming was in an IT class in high school, when I had to program a 'car' - which was nothing more than a triangle - to travel a particular route. I liked the reasoning behind it but I thought of it as a game with a very old-fashioned interface and I didn’t see the link with programming in real life. So that was it for then.

In the summer of 2019 I went for a drink with an ex-colleague, we had both been working as a work simplifier at Colruyt. After I left the company she changed roles and now worked as a business architect. I was triggered by her job description and I started searching for information about the different roles that exist in IT.

The role of functional analist immediately appealed to me. I like the combination of analysis and communication, building a bridge between the business and the software team, working together towards a common goal. I like things to move forward so I really look forward to working in a fast evolving industry.

I’m very happy that I’m given the opportunity to work as a functional analist for FPS Finance. I'm excited to contribute to projects with added social value and to be part of a very diverse team.
